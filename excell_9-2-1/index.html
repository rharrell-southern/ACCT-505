Johnson Company


In recent years, Johnson Company has purchased three machines. Because of frequent employee turnover in the accounting department, a different accountant was in charge of selecting the depreciation method for each machine, and various methods have been used. Information concerning the machines is summarized in the table below.

Machine Acquired Cost Salvage Value Useful Life (in years) Deprecation Method
1 Jan. 1, Year 1 $76,000 $10,000 8 Straight-line
2 July 1, Year 2 85,000 10,000 5 Declining-balance
3 Oct. 1, Year 3 66,000 5,000 6 Units-of-activity

For the declining-balance method, Johnson Company uses the double-declining rate. For the units-of-activity method, total machine hours are expected to be 40,000. Actual hours of use in the first 3 years were: year 3, 1000; year 4, 6,500; and year 5, 8,000


Instructions:
  • Compute the amount of accumulated depreciation on each machine at the end of year 5.
